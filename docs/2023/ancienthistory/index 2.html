About Us

We are a team of junior doctors who are dedicated to changing the way medical students study for exams.

Our mission is to empower medical students to use the evidence-based principles of active recall, spaced repetition and concept mapping to maximise recall of content, helping students achieve outstanding exam results while retaining this information for use in clinical practice

We believe that the vast majority of medical students in the UK are not studying effectively, investing long periods of study time to tasks which will have a negligible impact on their performance on exam day. At MedicRecall, we want to change this.

Currently students at schools and universities are taught what content to learn but not how to learn it. Therefore, there is a large disconnect between cutting edge cognitive science research into effective studying techniques and what is being used in practice. Over the last decade there is now a consensus that there are 3 methods proven to be most effective at maximising understanding and exam performance: active recall, spaced repetition and concept mapping.You can see the difference these methods make on exam performance from the graph below (Karpincke et al. ). Repeated study, concept mapping and active recall practice each almost doubled performance on exam performance. Imagine if you could harness the power of all three techniques in the way you revise!

Therefore, our aim is make employing these techniques as easy as possible for students. How can we do this? By setting up a comprehensive platform with pre-made, verified content delivered in a format which can tap into the benefits of each one of these methods.

SJT Course

The SJT is a hugely important exam which is given equal importance to your entire academic performance during medical school in determining which foundation year jobs you are allocated to. We have targeted this exam as our team have found that this is the exam where evidence-based revision methods could make the biggest impact in how medical students prepare.

For our SJT course we have split each of the 8 key domains tested in this exam into decks of flashcards. Each deck is further subdivided into common topics and themes to help users gain an understanding of what is examined in the SJT meaning users can identify which category a question may testing (concept mapping). Additionally we deliver our peer-reviewed content in flashcard format with a unique algorithm which will prompt users to review the content that they found most challenging at spaced intervals. This content can be reviewed on our desktop site or our mobile application so that students can fit extra SJT revision in-between a busy life on clinical rotations.

We also appreciate that the SJT is a subjective exam meaning that official content produced by the UKFPO is the most valuable resource you should use. Therefore, we factor reviewing these mock papers into your personalised revision timetable and also provide a unofficial mark scheme to the additional paper available on the PearsonVue site (very useful to prompt discussion around these scenarios with your peers and master the principles tested in the SJT). As we are in our first year, we offer all our content completely free and only ask that users give us feedback on how we can improve our service.

This approach is a stark contrast to what is currently offered by SJT course providers. They offer a one-off, day-long lectures (an example of low-yield, passive leaning) alongside a bank of practice quesitons for prices up to £100.

Therefore, we have created the most comprehensive and only evidence-based revision course on the market to help student achieve their 1st choice job while also changing the way you study in the process! All for Free!
